The U.S. Preventive Services Task Force recommends that adults aged
40 to 75 years who have one or more cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk
factors and an estimated 10-year CVD risk of 10% or greater initiate
a statin for the primary prevention of CVD. This is a Grade B
recommendation.

Adults aged 40 to 75 years with one or more CVD risk factors and a
10-year CVD risk of 7.5% to less than 10% may selectively be offered
a statin. This is a Grade C recommendation; the net benefit is small.

CVD risk factors in the underlying evidence base include
dyslipidemia (LDL-C greater than 130 mg/dL or HDL-C less than
40 mg/dL), diabetes, hypertension, and smoking. The 10-year CVD risk
estimate is calculated using the AHA/ACC Pooled Cohort Equations.

For adults 76 years or older, the current evidence is insufficient
(Grade I) to assess the balance of benefits and harms of initiating a
statin for primary prevention.
