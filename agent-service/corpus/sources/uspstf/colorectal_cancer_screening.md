The U.S. Preventive Services Task Force recommends screening for
colorectal cancer in all adults aged 45 to 75 years. This is a Grade B
recommendation. Adults aged 76 to 85 years should be screened
selectively based on overall health, prior screening history, and
patient preference (Grade C).

Acceptable screening strategies include high-sensitivity guaiac
fecal occult blood test (gFOBT) or fecal immunochemical test (FIT)
annually, multitarget stool DNA every 1 to 3 years, CT colonography
every 5 years, flexible sigmoidoscopy every 5 years (or every 10
years with annual FIT), and colonoscopy every 10 years.

Screening reduces colorectal cancer incidence and mortality. The net
benefit is substantial for adults aged 50 to 75, moderate for adults
aged 45 to 49, and small for adults aged 76 to 85.
