The U.S. Preventive Services Task Force recommends annual screening
for lung cancer with low-dose computed tomography in adults aged 50
to 80 years who have a 20 pack-year smoking history and currently
smoke or have quit within the past 15 years. This is a Grade B
recommendation.

Screening should be discontinued once a person has not smoked for 15
years or develops a health problem that substantially limits life
expectancy or the ability or willingness to have curative lung
surgery.

Compared to the prior 2013 recommendation, the 2021 update lowered
the starting age from 55 to 50 and the pack-year threshold from 30
to 20. The change was driven by evidence that screening reduces lung
cancer mortality at lower exposure thresholds, particularly among
women and Black adults who tend to develop lung cancer at lower
cumulative tobacco exposure.
