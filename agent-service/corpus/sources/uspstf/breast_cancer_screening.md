title: Breast cancer screening (USPSTF)

date: 2024-04-30

The U.S. Preventive Services Task Force recommends biennial screening
mammography for women aged 40 to 74 years. This is a Grade B
recommendation. The evidence is insufficient to assess the balance of
benefits and harms of screening mammography in women aged 75 years or
older (Grade I).

Biennial (every 2 years) screening offers a more favorable
benefit-to-harm balance than annual screening for most women in this
age range, though both intervals reduce breast cancer mortality.
Women with a personal or family history suggestive of higher-risk
inherited cancer syndromes should be referred for genetic counseling.

The recommendation does not apply to women with prior breast cancer,
high-risk lesions, prior chest radiation, or known BRCA1/BRCA2
mutations.
