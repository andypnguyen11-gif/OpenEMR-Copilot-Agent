The U.S. Preventive Services Task Force recommends screening for
hypertension in adults 18 years or older with office blood pressure
measurement. This is a Grade A recommendation. The recommendation
also calls for confirmation of high blood pressure outside the
clinical setting before starting treatment.

Acceptable confirmation methods include 24-hour ambulatory blood
pressure monitoring (the reference standard) or home blood pressure
monitoring. Out-of-office measurement reduces misclassification
caused by white-coat hypertension and improves cardiovascular
outcomes when used to guide therapy.

Adults aged 18 to 39 years at average risk should be rescreened every
3 to 5 years; adults 40 years or older or with risk factors should be
rescreened annually. Risk factors include overweight or obesity,
Black race, and family history of hypertension.
