For adults aged 40 to 59 years with a 10-year cardiovascular disease
risk of 10 percent or greater, the decision to initiate low-dose
aspirin for the primary prevention of cardiovascular disease should
be individualized. The net benefit of aspirin use in this group is
small (Grade C). Persons who are not at increased risk for bleeding
and who are willing to take low-dose aspirin daily are more likely
to benefit.

For adults 60 years or older, the U.S. Preventive Services Task
Force recommends against initiating low-dose aspirin for the primary
prevention of cardiovascular disease (Grade D). The risk of major
bleeding offsets cardiovascular benefit at this age.

These recommendations apply to primary prevention only. They do not
apply to patients with established cardiovascular disease (history
of myocardial infarction, ischemic stroke, or angiographic coronary
disease), for whom secondary prevention with aspirin remains
standard of care.
