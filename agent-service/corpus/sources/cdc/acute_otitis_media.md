Acute otitis media is diagnosed by acute onset of symptoms, presence
of middle-ear effusion, and signs of middle-ear inflammation
(moderate-to-severe bulging of the tympanic membrane or otorrhea not
caused by otitis externa). Mild bulging plus recent ear pain, or
intense erythema of the tympanic membrane, also satisfy the
inflammation criterion when otalgia is present.

Initial antibiotic therapy is recommended for children aged 6 months
or older with severe symptoms (moderate-to-severe otalgia, otalgia for
at least 48 hours, or temperature 39°C or higher) and for children
younger than 24 months with bilateral disease. For children aged 6
months to 23 months with non-severe unilateral disease, and children
24 months or older with non-severe disease in either ear, a 48 to 72
hour observation period with shared decision making and a safety-net
prescription is acceptable.

First-line antibiotic is amoxicillin 80 to 90 mg per kg per day,
divided into two doses, when the patient has not received amoxicillin
in the prior 30 days, has no concurrent purulent conjunctivitis, and
has no history of recurrent otitis media unresponsive to amoxicillin.
Amoxicillin-clavulanate at the same amoxicillin component dose is
preferred when any of those conditions apply.

Treatment duration is 10 days for children younger than 2 years and
those with severe symptoms, 7 days for children aged 2 to 5 years with
non-severe disease, and 5 to 7 days for children 6 years or older with
non-severe disease. Adults with acute otitis media are treated with
amoxicillin or amoxicillin-clavulanate at standard adult dosing for 5
to 7 days; penicillin allergy alternatives include cefdinir,
cefuroxime, or — in true non-anaphylactic reactions — azithromycin,
acknowledging higher pneumococcal resistance to macrolides.

Follow-up is guided by symptom course rather than fixed visit
schedules; reassess at 48 to 72 hours when initial observation was
elected, when symptoms persist or worsen, or when the diagnosis is in
doubt.
