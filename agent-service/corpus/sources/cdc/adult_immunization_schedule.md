CDC recommends that all adults receive an annual influenza vaccine,
ideally in September or October. All licensed influenza vaccines
appropriate for the patient's age and health status are acceptable.

Pneumococcal vaccination is recommended for adults 65 years or older
and for adults 19 to 64 years with certain medical conditions
(chronic heart, lung, or liver disease; diabetes; immunocompromise;
cigarette smoking). Either PCV20 alone or PCV15 followed by PPSV23
is acceptable; current preference favors PCV20 for simplicity.

Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, acellular pertussis) is given once in
adulthood, then a Td or Tdap booster every 10 years. Pregnant
patients should receive Tdap during each pregnancy at 27 to 36
weeks' gestation.

Recombinant zoster vaccine (Shingrix) is recommended for adults 50
years or older as a 2-dose series, regardless of prior shingles or
prior live zoster vaccine. The series is also recommended for
immunocompromised adults 19 years or older.

COVID-19 vaccination is recommended for all adults; the current
season's updated formulation should be used.
